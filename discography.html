Kanye West

West's journey began with the release of his debut album, "The College Dropout", in February 2004. The album debuted at number two on the Billboard 200 chart and was later certified double-platinum by the RIAA. Building on his success, West dropped his second studio album, "Late Registration", in August 2005. The album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 with impressive first-week sales of 860,000 copies. It ranked as the ninth best-selling album of 2005 in the United States.

In September 2007, West unveiled his third studio album, "Graduation", which swiftly claimed the number one spot on the Billboard 200. The album sold a staggering 957,000 units in its first week, setting the record as the fastest-selling album in the US since "Late Registration" and breaking the record for most digital albums sold in a week.

"808s & Heartbreak", West's fourth studio album released in November 2008, continued his chart-topping streak by debuting at number one on the Billboard 200. The album showcased West's artistic growth and experimental sound, resonating with fans and critics alike.

In November 2010, West unleashed his critically acclaimed fifth studio album, "My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy", which debuted at number one on the Billboard 200. It achieved the fourth-highest digital first-week sales ever, solidifying West's place as a creative force in the industry. Collaborating with Jay-Z, West released the highly anticipated "Watch the Throne" in August 2011, setting a new record for US iTunes first-week sales.

West's label, GOOD Music, released the compilation album "Cruel Summer" in September 2012, which debuted at the top of the Top R&B/Hip-Hop Albums chart. His sixth studio album, "Yeezus", arrived in June 2013 and achieved widespread success, debuting at number one in multiple countries, including the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand.

"The Life of Pablo," released in February 2016, made history by becoming the first album to predominantly top the Billboard 200 through streaming. It also garnered the second-highest number of first-week streams for an album. West continued to make waves in the music industry with the release of his eighth studio album, "Ye", and his collaborative album with Kid Cudi, "Kids See Ghosts", both dropping in June 2018.

In October 2019, West unveiled his ninth studio album, "Jesus Is King", which marked a significant departure from his previous works by focusing on gospel themes. The album topped both the Top Christian Albums and Top Gospel Albums charts, showcasing West's versatility and artistic range.

West's tenth solo studio album, "Donda", released in 2021, achieved remarkable success. It recorded the second-biggest first-week performance in Spotify's history and equaled the record for the most consecutive number one debuts on the Billboard 200, alongside Eminem.

In February 2022, West surprised fans with "Donda 2", released exclusively on the Stem Player. This demonstrated his innovative approach to music distribution and further showcased his ability to captivate audiences with his ever-evolving artistry.

Throughout his prolific career, Kanye West has continually pushed boundaries, experimented with sounds, and amassed a devoted fan base. His discography is a testament to his influence and impact on the music industry, solidifying his position as one of the most prominent and groundbreaking artists of his generation.
